📊 The Excel Tracker

A spreadsheet that runs itself — type a vehicle in, and the dashboards recalculate automatically.

The Excel workbook is the same business modeled in a spreadsheet. There’s one place to type — the Data tab — and two dashboards (Inventory and Sales) that update themselves through formulas. Nothing is hand-totaled.

The three tabs

TabWhat it does
DataWhere you enter each vehicle: make, model, buy/sell price, dates, buyer, and the two partners with an auto-calculating cash %.
Sales DashboardKPI cards, monthly profit, profit by make, most-sold models, partner contributions, and a chart.
Inventory DashboardWhat you currently hold, capital tied up, days held, and a live list.

How it was made

  1. One source of truth. Every number on the dashboards points back to the Data tab with a formula, so editing data updates everything at once.
  2. Status & profit are automatic. A vehicle becomes “Sold” the moment a sell price is entered; Profit = Sell − Buy and days-to-sell compute themselves.
  3. Lookups, not copy-paste. The inventory list is pulled with INDEX/MATCH; the dashboards summarize with SUMIFS, COUNTIFS, and AVERAGEIFS.
  4. Partner split math. Each partner’s cash defaults to half the buy price and the % is a live formula that re-balances if you change an amount.
  5. Zero errors. Formulas are guarded so blank rows never throw #VALUE! or #DIV/0! — the file was tested to recalculate clean.
